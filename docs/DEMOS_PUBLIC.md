## Demo 1 — The Digital Dokan

**Essential digital identity. Zero ongoing admin work.**

This tier gives Art-Teas-Tree a beautiful, cinematic web presence and a frictionless QR menu system. It is the smallest commitment we offer, and it is still a complete product.

### What's included

- **Full landing page** in the Retro Kolkata aesthetic — Hero, Philosophy, the full Manifesto, the Boipara (Books) corner, and Footer.
- **Dynamic QR menu** — printed QR codes for each table that open a mobile-optimised menu when scanned.
- **Google Sheets CMS for the menu** — you edit a private Google Sheet from your phone. Change a price, mark something out of stock, add a new tea — the website updates within seconds. No code, no admin panel, no dependency on us.
- **Mobile-first design** — the menu is built for the small screen first, then scaled up.
- **Basic SEO** — meta tags, social cards (Open Graph), sitemap so the cafe shows up in Google search.

### What's not included

- Artist directory or Natyamancha section
- Form submissions, online ordering, reservations
- Email capture or newsletter
- Photo galleries beyond the cafe photos already in the design

### Investment

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| One-time build | **₹10,000** |
| Domain (annual, paid to registrar) | ~₹800–1,200 |
| Hosting | Free tier (Vercel/Netlify) |
| Ongoing admin work from you | Editing a Google Sheet when needed |

### Why this costs what it does

Building a cinematic, hand-crafted website with the Retro Kolkata aesthetic is significantly more design-intensive than a templated cafe site. The price covers the custom frontend development, the cultural layering of the design, mobile responsiveness, the QR menu route, and the Google Sheets integration that turns your menu into a self-serve CMS. We hold this price low because we believe in the project — it is the foundation we want for every cafe we work with in Kolkata.

## Demo 2 — The Ensemble *(our recommendation)*

**Everything in Demo 1, plus your artists become part of the website.**

This tier brings the Mime Institute's artist community onto the cafe's digital surface. It turns the website from a brochure into a noticeboard — a place where patrons learn about the people who perform and teach in your space.

### What's included

- **Everything in Demo 1** — landing page, QR menu, Sheets CMS.
- **Natyamancha (The Stage) section** — a masonry-style postcard grid integrated directly into the cafe's main site.
- **Unlimited artists** managed via Google Sheets. Five artists are featured prominently as vintage postcards on the main page; the rest appear inside a "See the full roster" popup that opens on the same page, showing every artist with their photo, craft, bio, and contact link.
- **Artist cards** — each featured artist has a photo, name, craft (mime, poet, musician, etc.), a short bio, and a contact link.
- **"Take the Stage" button** — interested artists can express interest via a contact form that emails the cafe directly.
- **Dual Google Sheets pipeline** — one tab for the menu, another for the artists. To rotate an artist out or add a new one, you simply edit the sheet. The site updates automatically.

### What's not included

- Search or filtering across artists (the full-roster popup is a scrollable list; for filters and search you would want Demo 3)
- Dedicated artist profile pages (each artist is a card or list entry, not a full page)
- Artist self-submission (artists still contact you; you decide who appears)
- A dedicated subdomain or web app for the artist directory (that is Demo 3)

### Investment

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| One-time build | **₹22,000** |
| Domain (annual, paid to registrar) | ~₹800–1,200 |
| Hosting | Free tier (Vercel/Netlify) |
| Ongoing admin work from you | Editing two Google Sheets when needed |

### Why this costs what it does

The artist section adds a second data pipeline, requires careful design for the postcard layout, the full-roster popup, and the contact modal, and involves heavier image-handling work (resizing artist photos for performance, lazy loading, optimising for mobile). The Sheets-to-website pipeline has to be more robust because two streams of content now flow through it. The price reflects roughly twice the build time of Demo 1, while keeping the same zero-admin promise.

### Why we recommend this tier

Of the three, Demo 2 best captures the *identity* of Art-Teas-Tree. The cafe is not just a tea stall — it is a stage. Without the artists visible on the website, the connection to the Mime Institute reads as a line in the footer. With them visible, the website becomes a true reflection of what the space is for. And because everything still runs on Google Sheets, you carry no operational burden.

If, in twelve to eighteen months, the artist directory outgrows ten slots or you start getting booking inquiries from outside Kolkata, we have a clear path to upgrade you to Demo 3 without rebuilding from scratch.

## Demo 3 — The Cultural Hub

**A dedicated, searchable artist platform. A web application, not a website.**

This tier separates the cafe's main site from a fully interactive artist directory hosted at its own subdomain (for example, `artists.artteastree.com`). It is the right choice if the artist community becomes a destination in its own right — if event organisers, venues, or patrons should be able to discover talent independently of the cafe.

### What's included

- **Everything in Demo 1** — landing page, QR menu, Sheets CMS for the menu.
- **A dedicated artist directory** at its own subdomain.
- **On the main cafe site**, the Natyamancha section becomes a beautifully designed redirect card that sends visitors to the directory.
- **Interactive search and filters** — patrons can find artists by craft (mime, musician, poet), language, availability, or location.
- **Unlimited artist profiles** — every artist gets a dedicated profile page with their full bio and portfolio.
- **Artist self-submission** — artists apply through a form with email verification; you approve them through an admin dashboard.
- **Custom admin dashboard** — you (or a Mime Institute representative) approve, edit, or remove artists from a clean, simple interface.
- **Image upload pipeline** — artists upload their own photos, which are automatically resized and optimised.

### Three sub-options within Demo 3

|---|---|---|
| Lean | **₹50,000** | Basic search with two filters, approval done through Supabase's built-in interface (no custom admin UI) |

| Full polish | **₹1,10,000** | Above + per-artist portfolio galleries, booking-request form for event organisers, analytics dashboard, Bengali/English UI toggle |

### Subscription (required for Demo 3)

A live web application has ongoing operational costs — hosting, the database, security updates, backups, occasional fixes. The subscription covers all of this so your platform stays online, fast, and secure without surprise bills.

### Why this costs what it does

Demo 3 is fundamentally a different category of product from Demos 1 and 2. The leap in price reflects:

- A real database (Supabase / PostgreSQL) replacing the Google Sheets CMS for the artist directory
- Authentication and approval workflows so artists can submit themselves safely
- A custom admin dashboard built specifically for your approval workflow
- Search indexing and filtering logic that performs well as the directory grows
- A secondary user interface (the directory itself) at a separate subdomain
- Ongoing operational responsibility on our end — uptime, backups, security patches

This is the price of moving from a website to a platform.

## Demo 4 — The Concierge

**Everything in Demo 3, plus an AI chatbot that books artists, answers menu questions, and acts as a 24/7 cultural guide — in English, Bengali, and Hindi.**

Demo 4 is the top of the ladder. It is the right choice when the cafe and the artist directory together become a destination — when event organisers want to discover artists at 2 AM, when visitors want to know what's vegan before they arrive, when patrons ask "what's adda?" and the cafe wants to answer them warmly without staff lifting a finger.

### What's included

- **Everything in Demo 3** (Recommended sub-tier) as the base.
- **A floating chat widget on every page** of both the main cafe site and the artist directory subdomain. The widget is designed in the cafe's aesthetic — it does not feel like a generic SaaS bot bolted onto your site.
- **Artist booking flow** — a visitor says "I need a mime artist for a corporate event in December", the chatbot asks the right follow-up questions, surfaces matching artists from your directory with their availability, captures the visitor's contact details, and forwards the lead to you.
- **Menu questions answered** — "what's vegan?", "do you have anything without caffeine?", "what should I try if I like ginger?" — answered automatically from your live menu.
- **Cafe FAQ** — opening hours, location, parking, group bookings, dietary needs, private events. The chatbot knows the answers so you don't have to keep typing them out.
- **Cultural concierge** — visitors curious about adda, lyadh, bhar, or the Mime Institute connection get warm, accurate explanations in the cafe's voice. This is your story, told at scale.
- **Trilingual conversation** — English, Bengali (Bangla script), and Hindi (Devanagari script). The bot replies in whatever language the visitor uses.
- **Lead capture** — every artist booking inquiry, every private-event request, every reservation intent is logged and emailed to you.
- **Owner conversation dashboard** — you can see every chat that happens. This is a free demand-research tool: what are people asking about? What artists are most in demand? What is your audience curious about?
- **Reservation intent capture** — "table for 4 on Saturday at 7" — the chatbot collects the request and sends it to you. (Note: this captures the *intent*; live availability checking requires a separate POS integration.)

### Three sub-options within Demo 4

| Sub-option | Investment | Difference |
|---|---|---|
| Lean | **₹1,15,000** | English-only, capped at 300 conversations/month, simpler widget, no owner dashboard |
| **Recommended** | **₹1,35,000** | Trilingual (English/Bengali/Hindi), 1,000 conversations/month, owner dashboard, full lead capture, cultural FAQ |
| Full polish | **₹1,70,000** | Above + voice input for visitors who prefer to speak, sentiment analytics, monthly performance reports, custom persona tuning |

### Subscription (required for Demo 4)

Running an AI chatbot has real, ongoing costs — the AI provider charges per conversation, the database keeps growing, and the chatbot's quality needs occasional tuning. The subscription covers all of this so the experience stays sharp and responsive.

| Tier | Monthly | What's covered |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | **₹3,499/mo** | All Demo 3 Standard benefits + AI usage capped at 1,000 conversations/month + monthly chatbot transcripts report |
| **Pro** *(recommended for Demo 4)* | **₹5,999/mo** | Above + uncapped conversations + quarterly chatbot tuning + sentiment analytics dashboard + 24-hour priority support |
| Enterprise | **₹9,999/mo** | Above + custom integrations (POS, calendar, CRM), monthly performance review call, dedicated account manager |

### Why this costs what it does

Demo 4 is genuinely a new layer of product. The investment reflects:

- A floating, mobile-friendly chatbot widget designed in the cafe's aesthetic — not a stock SaaS component
- Connecting the chatbot to a real AI model (Claude Haiku 4.5) with streaming responses so conversations feel natural
- The ability to ground every answer in your real menu and real artist directory — no AI-invented dishes or fictional artists
- Multilingual prompt engineering and testing in English, Bengali, and Hindi
- A conversation logging system and an owner dashboard so you see what your audience is asking
- Lead capture and email forwarding so booking inquiries reach you the moment they happen
- Hardening against abuse and prompt manipulation
- Ongoing responsibility for the AI's quality, uptime, and cost — handled inside the subscription

### Who Demo 4 is right for

Demo 4 is the right answer if **your artists become a destination in their own right** — if event organisers, wedding planners, corporate booking agents, or out-of-Kolkata patrons start finding the cafe through the artist directory and want to engage at scale. It is also right for cafes that see the chatbot as a cultural extension of the space, not just a support tool.

Demo 4 is **not the right answer** if you mostly want a beautiful website and a working QR menu. For that, Demos 1 or 2 are a better fit, both in cost and in operational simplicity.

## Upgrade path

The technical architecture is built so you can grow between tiers without losing work. If you choose Demo 1 today and want to add the artist section in six months, you do not rebuild the site — we extend it.

```
Demo 1  →  Demo 2  →  Demo 3  →  Demo 4
₹10,000     +₹15,000    +₹60,000    +₹55,000
            upgrade     upgrade     upgrade
```

Upgrades carry forward all the work done in the previous tier. Demo 2 → Demo 3 credits the artist work; Demo 3 → Demo 4 layers the chatbot onto your existing directory without rebuilding anything underneath. The subscription updates accordingly when you upgrade into a tier that requires one.

## A small glossary

For team members or stakeholders less familiar with the design language we have used:

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *Adda* (আড্ডা) | Bengali tradition of long, agenda-less conversation. The cafe's central value. |
| *Cha er dokan* (চায়ের দোকান) | Roadside tea stall. The cultural reference the cafe's design is built on. |
| *Bhar* (ভাঁড়) | The disposable clay cup used for tea in Kolkata. A recurring visual motif in the design. |
| *Boipara* (বইপাড়া) | The book district — College Street in Kolkata. The "Books Corner" section is themed around this. |
| *Natyamancha* (নাট্যমঞ্চ) | The theatre stage. Name we have given the artist section. |
| *Reel* (রিল) | The current photo gallery section, designed as a director's camera viewfinder. |
| CMS | Content Management System — for Demos 1 and 2, this is simply Google Sheets. |
