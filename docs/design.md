## Problem Statement

Art Teas Tree Cafe is a Kolkata café tied to the Mime Institute of Calcutta. It reimagines the roadside *cha er dokan* as an artistic social space — books, tea, conversation, smoke, mime, theatre, friendship. The website needs to translate that physical-world warmth into a web experience that feels Bengali, intimate, intellectual, and analog — not a glossy startup café site, not luxury branding, not sterile minimalism.

The brand promise: **human connection over digital isolation.** The website has to embody that even though it lives on a screen.

## What Makes This Cool

The category is dominated by two tropes: glossy white-space minimalism and over-styled lifestyle photography. Both feel imported. Neither feels Kolkata. The cool thing here is making a website that feels like a Bengali little magazine — typeset by hand, slightly imperfect, full of marginalia and warmth — while still being a fast, modern, accessible piece of software. If executed, it sits in a category of one for café sites in this market.

## Constraints

- **Audience loads on Kolkata mobile data** — perf budget matters. Target <100KB JS, <1MB total page weight including hero image.
- **Content is currently absent.** No real photos uploaded yet. No final menu copy. The build must work with curated documentary stock as stand-ins, designed so real photos drop in later without redesign.
- **Tech direction (revised from brief):** Astro + React islands + Tailwind, static export. The brief said React + Tailwind; Astro preserves React where it earns its weight (menu filter, gallery lightbox) while shipping a fraction of the JavaScript.
- **Distribution is web-first.** Hosting on Vercel or Netlify free tier. Custom domain TBD by client.
- **Mime Institute association is meaningful but not the headline.** The café stands on its own; the institute connection is a credit, not a co-brand.

## Premises (all confirmed by user)

1. **Five sections, not eight.** The brief's eight sections fragment the emotional arc. Compress: Hero → Adda Philosophy (about + community merged) → The Experience (experience + book corner merged) → The Menu → Find Us (gallery folded into Adda page; footer carries location and Instagram).
2. **Astro + React islands beats React-only SPA** for a content marketing site loading on Indian mobile networks. Static HTML where possible, React only where interactivity earns it.
3. **Restrained ink-on-paper motion replaces "cinematic transitions."** No parallax, no swooping reveals. Page transitions are slow ink-fades (250–400ms). Hovers are subtle (a teastain bloom, a typewriter caret on the tagline). Restraint IS the aesthetic; cinematic motion would betray it.
4. **Smoking-friendly framing stays but is flagged.** Cigarettes are honest to adda culture. But Google Ads, Meta Ads, and some delivery platforms restrict explicit promotion of smoking. If paid acquisition or delivery-platform listings are ever in scope, soften copy to "open-air seating, no judgment zone" and similar. Site copy should keep the spirit; the marketing surface area can be tuned later.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Single-page poster (rejected — too forgettable)
One long scrolling page, ~5 sections, Astro static. Ships in days. Loads instantly. Looks like every other thoughtful small-business site. Doesn't earn the "Satyajit Ray" ambition the brief reaches for. Right call only if launch needs to happen this week.

### Approach B: Editorial multi-page (CHOSEN)
Five pages, each a magazine spread. Astro + React islands + Tailwind. Slow ink-fade page transitions. Static export. ~1 week of human work, ~45 min of CC implementation time. Best long-term trajectory — events, blog, mime-institute collaborations all slot in cleanly later.

### Approach C: The Adda Journal — site as hand-bound journal (rejected — higher risk than the moment calls for)
Page-turn physics, marginalia, scrapbook compositions. Genuinely earns "if Satyajit Ray designed it." But build cost is real, accessibility risk on handwritten fonts at small sizes, and one bad demo on a slow phone collapses the illusion. Worth revisiting in a v2 redesign once the brand has shipped and grown.

## Recommended Approach: Editorial Multi-Page (Approach B)

### Site map

| Route | Purpose | Hero artifact |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| `/` | Hero + philosophy excerpt + 3 teaser cards into other pages | Tagline poster |
| `/experience` | Long-form essay on adda, books, conversation, mime culture | Full-bleed documentary photo |
| `/menu` | Hand-laid menu — Teas, Coffees, Snacks, Quick Bites | Tea-stained menu card grid |
| `/adda` | Gallery + community + book corner folded together | Masonry photo essay |
| `/visit` | Location, hours, contact, Instagram embed, footer | Hand-drawn map sketch |

Note: `/adda` absorbs three of the brief's original sections (Gallery, Book Corner, Community). They share a visual register (documentary, candid, atmospheric) and reading them as one page makes the emotional weight land harder than three short ones.

### Visual system

**Palette** (working defaults; will be re-derived from real photos when they arrive):
- `#1c1410` charcoal-black (body text, ink)
- `#3b2418` deep tea (primary brand)
- `#7a4a2a` warm tea (accents, links)
- `#c9a87a` evening lamp (highlights, warm hover states)
- `#e8dcc4` aged paper (backgrounds)
- `#f4ecd8` cream paper (page background)
- `#5a6b3e` muted moss green (secondary accent — for tags, dividers)

**Typography**:
- **Display serif:** *Cormorant Garamond* or *EB Garamond* (free, editorial, slightly literary)
- **Body serif:** *Lora* or *Source Serif 4* (readable at small sizes, warm)
- **Handwritten accent:** *Caveat* or *Homemade Apple* (used SPARINGLY — a tagline, a marginal note, never body copy)
- **Bengali script:** *Hind Siliguri* or *Noto Serif Bengali* — for occasional Bengali words ("আড্ডা", "চা") used as visual punctuation, not full sentences

**Texture**:
- A subtle paper-grain SVG noise overlay (1–3% opacity) on page backgrounds. NOT animated. Just present.
- Section dividers are hand-drawn ink lines (SVG, ~6KB) — not flat hairlines.
- Photos get a faint sepia + grain filter unless the original photo is already warm.

**Motion**:
- Page transition: 300ms ink-fade-up on enter (mask-image with a torn-paper edge, fading from bottom).
- Hover on links: 200ms warm-amber underline that draws left-to-right, like a fountain pen.
- Hero tagline: typewriter caret blinks once, then steadies. No looping animation.
- No parallax. No scroll-jacking. No reveal-on-scroll for body copy (it's annoying and bad for screen readers).

### Component inventory

Astro components (server-rendered, zero JS by default):
- `Layout.astro` — paper background, nav, footer, font preload
- `Nav.astro` — minimal nav, the cafe name on the left, 4 page links on the right
- `Footer.astro` — location, hours, Instagram link, hand-drawn signature
- `Section.astro` — wrapper for editorial sections with ink-line divider above
- `MenuCard.astro` — for the menu page; one card per item with name, price, optional 1-line note
- `Polaroid.astro` — image with tape, slight rotation, optional handwritten caption
- `Pullquote.astro` — large serif quote with hanging punctuation, like a literary magazine

React islands (only where interaction earns it):
- `MenuFilter.tsx` — filter chips for Teas / Coffees / Snacks / Quick Bites; client:visible
- `GalleryLightbox.tsx` — click photo, see larger; client:idle
- `InstagramFeed.tsx` (optional) — pulls latest 6 posts from a server-side cache; client:visible

### Page-by-page detail

**`/` (Home)**
- Hero: full-viewport, paper texture, large hand-set serif tagline. Recommended tagline: **"Where conversations steep slowly."** Below it, in handwritten accent: *Adda. Art. Tea.* CTA buttons: "Visit Us" (primary), "See the Menu" (secondary).
- Below the fold: a 3-paragraph excerpt from the philosophy (~150 words). Then three magazine-style teaser cards leading to /experience, /menu, /adda. Each card is a polaroid + a one-line invitation in serif.
- Footer-area pull-quote in Bengali: a single line of adda-themed verse or a Tagore fragment. Set huge.

**`/experience`**
- Long-form essay split into 4 movements: *Cha er Dokan* (the inspiration), *The Books* (the corner), *The Smoke* (the social register), *The Hours* (slow living). Each movement opens with a full-bleed photo and a serif drop-cap.
- ~600–800 words total. This is the soul page. Skim-able structure but rewards reading.
- One inline pullquote per movement.

**`/menu`**
- Four sections: Teas (the heart), Coffees, Snacks, Quick Bites.
- Each item: name in serif, 1-line description in italic, price in tabular monospace.
- Menu cards laid out as if pasted onto a single tea-stained page. Subtle rotation per card (-1° to 1°), fixed on hover.
- Filter chips above (React island) — toggleable on mobile, always-visible on desktop.

**`/adda`**
- Opens with a 60-word framing paragraph: what *adda* means, what gathers here.
- Then a masonry gallery of 12–18 documentary photos — candid, slightly imperfect. Click for lightbox.
- Below: a "Book Corner" sub-section — bookshelf-style horizontal scroll of book spines, each one a real book in the café (when content is ready). Until then, a placeholder of 8 well-chosen literary classics relevant to the brand.
- Below: 3–5 "voices from the café" testimonials, set as if torn from a notebook, each in slightly different handwriting. Real quotes when available; thoughtfully written placeholders otherwise.

**`/visit`**
- Location with a hand-drawn map sketch (SVG, custom — Park Street, College Street, or wherever the actual café sits).
- Hours, in serif.
- Phone, email, Instagram handle.
- A small "End of magazine" signature at the bottom — a hand-drawn flourish, the cafe logo small, and a single Bengali line as the closing note.

## Open Questions

These don't block the build but should be resolved before launch:

1. **Real photos.** Build will use curated stock (Unsplash with proper attribution; preference for photographers with documentary Kolkata work). Real photos should replace stock before public launch — design will accommodate either.
2. **Real menu copy and prices.** The menu page is structured but needs actual items and prices from the café owner. Placeholder menu uses representative items (masala chai, lebu cha, single-origin filter coffee, ghugni, Kolkata-style cutlets, etc.) until then.
3. **Exact location.** Currently a placeholder. Need actual address for the map sketch and `/visit` page.
4. **Instagram handle.** Need the live handle to wire the embed and the footer link.
5. **Logo.** No logo yet. The build will use a hand-set serif wordmark as a working logo. If a real logo exists or is in design, slot it in to replace.
6. **Bengali typography review.** The Bengali fragments should be reviewed by a native reader before launch — even small typesetting errors in Bengali script read poorly.

## Success Criteria

- Lighthouse performance score ≥ 90 on mobile (3G simulation).
- LCP < 2.5s on 4G mobile from a Kolkata IP.
- All five pages reachable via keyboard, screen-reader navigable.
- No layout shift on font load (proper `font-display: swap` + size-adjust).
- Renders correctly on iOS Safari, Android Chrome, desktop Chrome/Firefox/Safari.
- Visual consistency: no page looks like it was made by a different person than the others. Tone, type, spacing, motion all coherent.
- The "feel" test: a Kolkata adda regular looks at the site and says "yes, this is one of us" — not "this is a tech company's idea of us."

## Distribution Plan

- **Hosting:** Vercel or Netlify free tier (Astro deploys to either trivially).
- **Domain:** Client-owned. Suggest `artteastreecafe.com` or `.in` if available.
- **CI/CD:** GitHub Actions on push to `main` — runs build, deploys preview on PRs, deploys production on merge. Astro's default GitHub Action template is fine.
- **Analytics:** Plausible or Umami (privacy-respecting; no cookie banner needed). Avoid GA4 — overkill and erodes the privacy posture that matches the brand.
- **Content updates:** All content lives in Markdown / MDX files in the repo. Owner gets a one-page CONTRIBUTING.md showing how to edit menu prices and add gallery photos via GitHub web UI. No CMS yet — overkill at this scale.

## Dependencies

- Astro 4+ (latest stable as of build).
- Tailwind CSS via Astro integration.
- Two Google Fonts (or self-hosted): one display serif, one body serif, one handwritten accent, one Bengali serif. Self-host for perf and privacy.
- Optional: `astro-icon` for a small set of icons (Instagram, location pin, clock).
- Optional: `framer-motion` only if motion needs more than CSS can give. Strong preference for CSS first.

## Risks

- **Typography drift.** Editorial sites live or die on type discipline. The build needs a strict type scale (4–5 sizes max) and any contributor adding content must use semantic components, not raw HTML.
- **Stock photos that look generic.** Stand-ins must be hand-picked from documentary photographers, not generic café shots. A bad stock choice breaks the spell. Review every photo before accepting.
- **Bengali typesetting.** Get a native reader to review before launch.
- **Smoking framing in marketing surfaces.** Site copy can keep "smoke, conversation" — but if/when paid ads or delivery-platform listings happen, that copy has to be tuned.
- **Mobile motion.** Anything beyond CSS opacity/transform on mobile is a perf risk. If `framer-motion` enters the build, it must be loaded only on desktop or only on demand.

## What I noticed about how you think

- You said "a rainy evening conversation over tea and cigarettes." That is not a brand brief sentence — that's a memory or a specific evening. The website should keep aiming at *that* evening, not at the abstract concept of a café.
- You wrote a brief that explicitly listed what to *avoid* — "corporate café aesthetics, sterile minimalism, glossy luxury branding." Most briefs only describe what they want. Naming the anti-pattern is rarer and harder, and it's load-bearing for getting this right.
- You named "human connection over digital isolation" as the philosophy. That's a manifesto, not a tagline. It deserves its own page (the `/experience` page, here) and not just a hero callout. The site is structured to honor that.
- You ended with "if Satyajit Ray designed it." That's the actual bar. Approach B serves that bar honorably; Approach C would have served it more ambitiously. If the project lives long enough to earn a v2, the journal-as-website idea is worth revisiting.

## Locked Architecture (post eng-review, 2026-05-11)

Eng review locked the following decisions. Implementation begins from this baseline.

### Stack
- **Astro 4** (static export, no server) + **Tailwind CSS 3** + **TypeScript**.
- **React 18** islands ONLY for `MenuFilter` and `GalleryLightbox`. Other pages ship zero JS.
- **`@fontsource`** packages for self-hosted fonts (no Google Fonts CDN).
- **Astro Content Collections** for menu/gallery/books (type-safe via Zod).
- **Astro `<Image>`** component for all image references (auto AVIF/WebP/responsive).
- **Astro View Transitions** (built-in `<ViewTransitions />`) for the ink-fade page transitions. No Framer Motion needed.

### Hero (revised)
- **No video.** Homepage hero is a single optimized photograph with overlay typography. Removes ~2MB of asset weight, eliminates the mobile-LCP risk entirely, simplifies the build.

### Content schemas (Astro Content Collections)
```ts
menu: { name, category: 'tea'|'coffee'|'snack'|'quick-bite', price, description?, note?, available?, order? }
essays: { title, slug, drop_cap, body (markdown) }
```
Static manifests for gallery and books live in `src/data/` (simpler than collections for fixed lists).

### Component contracts (one-line each)
- `Layout({ title, description })` — wraps every page; injects `<ViewTransitions />`, fonts, paper bg.
- `Nav()` — renders 4 page links; current route highlighted.
- `Footer()` — location, hours, social, hand-drawn flourish.
- `Section({ heading?, accent?, divider? })` — editorial wrapper; optional ink-line divider above.
- `Polaroid({ src, alt, caption?, rotate?: number, tape?: 'top'|'corner' })` — Astro `<Image>` inside paper-frame styling.
- `PullQuote({ author?, lang?: 'en'|'bn' })` — large serif blockquote.
- `MenuCard({ name, price, description?, note? })` — single item; rotated -1°-1°, fixed on hover.
- `BookSpine({ title, author, color?: string })` — vertical book spine in the shelf.
- `MenuFilter({ initialCategory? })` — React island; filters `[data-menu-category]` items.
- `GalleryLightbox({ photos })` — React island; on-click opens larger photo.

### Performance budgets
- LCP < 2.5s on mobile 4G from a Kolkata IP.
- Total JS shipped < 30KB (gzipped) on `/` and `/experience` and `/visit` (zero islands). < 50KB on `/menu` and `/adda`.
- Each page < 1MB total weight including hero image.
- Lighthouse mobile perf ≥ 90, accessibility ≥ 95.

### CI
- `astro build` runs on every push to `main` via GitHub Actions. Catches type errors, missing imports, broken content frontmatter. Deploys via Vercel/Netlify GitHub integration when domain is wired.

### NOT in scope (deferred, deliberately)
- Lighthouse CI (add when we hit the first perf regression — premature now).
- Visual regression testing (Playwright screenshots) — too noisy for a marketing site that updates copy frequently.
- E2E tests — `astro build` succeeding on a static site catches ~95% of what unit tests would.
- A CMS (Sanity, Contentful, Storyblok). Markdown + git-based editing is enough for this scale.
- Internationalization. Bengali fragments are inline as styled spans, not a full i18n system.
- Newsletter signup, online ordering, reservation system. Not in the brief.
- Analytics. Plausible/Umami can be added in 2 lines when the site is ready to ship publicly.
- Sitemap.xml + robots.txt. Trivial — added at deploy time.

### What already exists
- `README.md` with the brand brief — referenced for copy generation but not included in the build.
- `docs/design.md` (this file) — drives the implementation.
- Nothing else. Greenfield.

### Test plan (lightweight by intent)
- **Build check:** `astro build` must succeed in CI on every push. Catches: TS errors, missing imports, broken content schemas, dead links via `astro:check`.
- **Manual QA after first build:** load each of 5 routes in dev server. Verify nav, footer, paper texture, fonts, all images resolve, view transitions work between pages.
- **Mobile spot-check:** open dev server in a phone-sized viewport (Chrome DevTools mobile emulation). Verify nothing horizontal-scrolls, fonts look right, photos are reasonably sized.
- Future: when content stabilizes, add a single Playwright smoke test that hits all 5 routes and asserts h1 exists. Not required to ship.

### Failure modes (and what catches them)
| Failure | Catch |
|---|---|
| Broken image import | `astro build` fails the CI |
| Menu MD missing required field | Content collection Zod validation fails the build |
| Font not loading | Visible during manual QA on first deploy preview |
| View transition tearing | Visible during manual QA, fall back to no-transition gracefully |
| Bengali glyph missing | Visible to a native reader before launch (the design doc's open question) |

Build is small enough that sequential implementation in one pass is faster than coordinating worktrees. ~75 min total CC time. Sequential.
